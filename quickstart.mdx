This guide takes you from an empty workspace to your first simulation result. It should take about an hour, most of which is the simulation running.

Simulant is a rigorous inference layer on observed human evidence — not AI roleplay. Composition is controlled before any language model speaks; uncertainty is measured; validity is never assumed. See [How grounding works](/data/grounding) if you want the methodology first.

## Prerequisites

- Access to Simulant at [app.simulant.tech](https://app.simulant.tech). If you don't have an account, [book a demo](https://simulant.tech/contact?intent=demo) or accept a workspace invite.
- An asset to test — a video, an image, or a block of copy. You can also start with messaging typed directly into a test step.

## Set up your workspace

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Sign up at [app.simulant.tech/signup](https://app.simulant.tech/signup) with your work email, then enter the 6-digit code we send you. If you were invited to a workspace, open the invite link instead and accept.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete onboarding">
    Tell us your name, role, organization, and what you plan to use Simulant for. This creates your first **workspace** — the shared space where your team's simulations, cohorts, and data live.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Follow the setup guide">
    New workspaces show a **Get started** checklist in the sidebar. It tracks four first-run steps — add a dataset, run your first simulation, upload an asset, and invite your team. Steps auto-complete as you do them, and the card retires itself once everything's checked off.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The sidebar is organised into **Research** (Simulations), **Library** (Cohorts, Datasets, Assets, Polls, Tests, Focus groups), and **Intelligence** (Agent, Reports, Memos). Workspace settings live under the workspace switcher at the top-left.
</Tip>

## Run your first simulation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add data (optional)">
    Simulant ships with a **public library** of grounding datasets under **Datasets → Browse**, so you can start immediately. To add your own signal, go to **Datasets → Add dataset**. See [Datasets](/data/datasets).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build a cohort">
    Go to **Cohorts → New cohort**. Describe the population in plain language, set the region and characteristics, and choose a cohort size. Simulant shows the implied precision as you adjust. See [Build a cohort](/guides/cohorts).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload an asset (optional)">
    Go to **Assets → Upload asset** and drop in your video or image. For copy tests, you can skip this and type messaging directly into a test step. See [Assets](/guides/creatives).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a simulation">
    Go to **Simulations → New simulation**. On the **Model** tab, add a **Test** (or **Poll**) node and wire it to your cohort. Configure the step — hypothesis, variants, metrics, questions — in the node panel. See [Simulations](/guides/simulations).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run it">
    Click **Run simulation**. Simulant executes every wired step and assembles the results. You'll get a notification when the run finishes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the results">
    Open the simulation's **Results** tab, select the run, and open the step readout. You'll see headline metrics (for tests), weighted shares (for polls), sentiment by segment, and tabs for **Verbatims**, **Themes**, and **Recommendations**.

    <Frame caption="Your first result — step readouts with segment cuts behind the numbers.">
      <img src="/images/app/test-results.png" alt="Simulation run results showing recall, sentiment, intent, clarity, and segment cuts" />
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Check>
  You've run your first simulation. Next, save a reusable [test](/guides/tests) or [poll](/guides/polls) in the Library, or add a [focus group step](/guides/focus-groups) on the same model.
</Check>
