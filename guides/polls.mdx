A **poll step** asks a representative panel a short set of questions and returns weighted, segmented results. It lives inside a [simulation](/guides/simulations) on the Model canvas. Add a **Poll** node, wire it to a **Cohort** node, and configure the questions in the node panel.

Where a [test step](/guides/tests) measures reactions to something you put in front of the panel, a poll step measures **what the panel already thinks**.

## When to add a poll step

Reach for a poll when the question stands on its own and you want a fielded readout:

- **Vote intention or ballot** — which candidate, which party, which option.
- **Approval and favorability** — how a person, brand, or policy is currently viewed.
- **Top issues or priorities** — what the panel says matters most.
- **Ratings and sentiment** — a 1–5 read on a policy, price point, or idea.
- **Open-ended pulse** — verbatims on a topic, clustered into themes.

If you need to compare messages, pre-test creative, or measure lift against a control, add a [test step](/guides/tests) instead.

## Configure a poll step

On a simulation's **Model** tab, click **Add node → Poll** and open the node panel.

### Identity

Name the poll and, optionally, state a **hypothesis** — what you expect the panel to say and why. Stating an expectation up front keeps the readout honest.

### Cohort

Wire the poll to a **Cohort** node on the canvas. Every question is answered by the same panel drawn from that cohort, so cross-tabs are consistent across questions. See [Build a cohort](/guides/cohorts).

### Context

Set the real-world environment the panel is answering within:

- **Current** — today's prevailing issues, mood, and events.
- **Neutral** — strip context out for a clean baseline.
- **Custom** — specify the issues, mood, events, and competitive pressure yourself.

You can also wire a **News** node for article context. Context is saved with the poll so the run can be reconstructed and validated later.

### Questions

Add the questions each persona answers. Poll question types are:

- **Single choice** — pick one option from a list you define.
- **Multi select** — pick any that apply.
- **Rating 1–5** — a numeric scale, reported as a weighted mean and distribution.
- **Yes / No** — binary, reported as a yes share with margin of error.
- **Open-ended** — free-text responses that become verbatims.

Drag to reorder. A tight poll mixes one or two structured questions with a single open-ended prompt.

### Fielding

- **Sample size** — how many personas to draw from the cohort (capped at the cohort's `n`), or **Max** to use every persona.

Every persona answers all questions in a single pass. There is no stimulus, no variants, and no control arm — results are weighted aggregates of what the panel thinks.

## Run it

Click **Run simulation** in the simulation header. Simulant fields the questions across the panel and streams progress as personas answer.

<Note>
  Runtime scales with sample size and question count. Larger panels take longer but tighten the margin of error on each reported share.
</Note>

## Read the results

Open the simulation's **Results** tab, select a run, and click the poll step. Per question you'll see:

- **Weighted shares** — each option's percentage, with `n` responded and the leading share highlighted.
- **Rating distribution** — for 1–5 questions, the weighted mean with the full distribution.
- **Margin of error** — reported at 95% on the leading share; questions where the top two options fall inside the margin are flagged **Within margin**.
- **By segment** — the same cut across the demographic and psychographic segments defined by the cohort.
- **Verbatims** — for open-ended questions, individual quotes grouped by segment.

The right rail shows run metadata — panel size, cohort, completion rate, effective sample size, and any confidence flags — alongside the hypothesis you stated.

<Tip>
  A poll where the leading share sits inside the margin of error is a genuine tie. Read the segment cuts and the verbatims — the panel-level number is not the story.
</Tip>

## Validate against reality

Poll results are stored per question so a real-world outcome — an election result, a published poll, an observed conversion rate — can be attached to the run later. That turns a fielded poll into a **predicted-versus-actual** check you can point to when defending the method.

Attach outcomes from the poll step's detail view once real-world numbers are available.
