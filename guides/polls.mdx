A **poll** is a reusable questionnaire you save under **Library → Polls**. Load it onto a [simulation](/guides/simulations) as a **Poll** step to field it against a cohort and read weighted, segmented results.

Where a [test](/guides/tests) measures reactions to something you put in front of the panel, a poll measures **what the panel already thinks**.

## When to use a poll

Reach for a poll when the question stands on its own and you want a fielded readout:

- **Vote intention or ballot** — which candidate, which party, which option.
- **Approval and favorability** — how a person, brand, or policy is currently viewed.
- **Top issues or priorities** — what the panel says matters most.
- **Ratings and sentiment** — a 1–5 read on a policy, price point, or idea.

If you need to compare messages, pre-test creative, or measure lift against a control, use a [test](/guides/tests) instead.

## Create a library poll

Go to **Library → Polls → New poll**. Name it, optionally state a **hypothesis**, and add questions:

- **Single choice** — pick one option from a list you define.
- **Multi select** — pick any that apply.
- **Rating 1–5** — a numeric scale.

Design fields (hypothesis, questions, metrics) live on the library instrument. Audience, context, and run settings are set when you place the poll on a simulation canvas.

## Use a poll in a simulation

On a simulation's **Model** tab, click **Add node → Poll**. You can configure the step from scratch or load a saved library poll. Wire it to a **Cohort** node for respondents, and optionally to **News** or **Knowledge** for context.

<Frame caption="The Model canvas — research steps (including polls) wired to a cohort.">
  <img src="/images/app/test-builder.png" alt="Simulation model canvas with cohort and research steps connected" />
</Frame>

## Read results

After you **Run simulation**, open the **Results** tab and select the poll step. You'll see weighted shares, margins, and segment cuts — the fielded percentages a traditional pollster would report.
