A **focus group step** convenes a small room of personas from a cohort and lets them talk. It lives inside a [simulation](/guides/simulations) on the Model canvas. Where a [test step](/guides/tests) collects reactions across the whole population, a focus group step shows you how a handful of people reason together — where they agree, where they push back, and what changes their mind.

## Add a focus group step

On a simulation's **Model** tab, click **Add node → Focus group** and open the node panel.

<Frame caption="The Model canvas — a focus group step wired to a cohort and optional asset node.">
  <img src="/images/app/focus-group-builder.png" alt="Simulation model canvas with a focus group node connected to a cohort and asset" />
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Name it and set the objective">
    Give the group a name and a sharp **discussion objective** — what you want to learn. The objective keeps the moderator on track.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a moderator">
    Pick an **AI moderator**, which runs instantly and probes every participant consistently, or a **Human moderator** to steer the room live. For the AI moderator, choose a style such as `probing`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach an asset (optional)">
    Wire an **Asset** node to have the room react to a video, image, or message. See [Assets](/guides/creatives).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write discussion prompts">
    Add the questions the moderator works through, and drag to reorder them. Open, specific prompts spark the best discussion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the room">
    Wire the step to a **Cohort** node, then select participants from its roster. Five to eight participants is the sweet spot — big enough for real dynamics, small enough to follow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set length and dynamics">
    Choose a session **length** (for example, `45 min`) and toggle group **dynamics**: allow **crosstalk**, encourage **dissent**, run **anonymous**, and generate a **summary**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Run it

Click **Run simulation** in the simulation header. The moderator opens with your first prompt and the participants respond, react to each other, and work toward — or away from — consensus.

<Frame caption="A focus group readout — the moderated transcript with live sentiment, participants, and emerging themes.">
  <img src="/images/app/focus-group-room.png" alt="Focus group results showing a moderated transcript, participant list, and live sentiment" />
</Frame>

## Read the discussion

Open the simulation's **Results** tab, select a run, and click the focus group step to follow:

- **The transcript** — the full back-and-forth, attributed to each participant.
- **Shifts** — where a participant changes position, and what moved them.
- **Summary** — the throughline of the conversation, the points of agreement, and the live dissent.

<Tip>
  Run a focus group step after a test step in the same simulation to understand the *why* behind a surprising number. Wire both steps to the same cohort node so the room reflects the population you just measured.
</Tip>
