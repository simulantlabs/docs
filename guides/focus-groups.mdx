A **focus group** is a reusable discussion guide you save under **Library → Focus groups**. Load it onto a [simulation](/guides/simulations) as a **Focus group** step to convene a small room from a cohort and watch it deliberate, react, and shift its mind.

Where a [test](/guides/tests) collects reactions across the whole population, a focus group shows how a handful of people reason together.

## Create a library focus group

Go to **Library → Focus groups → New focus group**. Set the design fields:

- **Name** and **objective** — what you want to learn
- **Moderator style** — warm and curious, neutral and probing, or challenging
- **Discussion prompts** — the questions the moderator works through
- **Length** and **participant count** — typically 45 minutes and 5–8 people
- **Dynamics** — crosstalk, dissent, anonymous, summary

Audience and asset attachments are set when you place the focus group on a simulation canvas.

## Use a focus group in a simulation

On a simulation's **Model** tab, click **Add node → Focus group**. Load a library guide or configure from scratch. Wire a **Cohort** for respondents and optionally an **Asset** for the room to react to.

<Frame caption="The Model canvas — a focus group step wired downstream of a test.">
  <img src="/images/app/focus-group-builder.png" alt="Simulation model canvas with cohort, test, and focus group nodes connected" />
</Frame>

## Read the room

After you **Run simulation**, open the **Results** tab and select the focus group step. You'll see the moderated transcript, participant shifts, and a summary of where the room landed.
