A **cohort** is the audience a simulation step runs against. It's a slice of the grounded population, defined by how you describe it and the filters you apply. Every cohort reports a respondent count (`n`) and an implied precision so you always know how many personas stand behind a result.

## Create a cohort

Go to **Cohorts → New cohort** and work down the builder. A preview panel on the right shows origin, status, and implied precision as you refine.

<Frame caption="The cohort builder — describe the population in plain language, set region and characteristics, and choose a target size.">
  <img src="/images/app/cohort-builder.png" alt="Cohort builder with population description, region, demographics, characteristics, and cohort size presets" />
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Describe the population">
    Under **Population**, write who you're trying to reach in plain language — for example, `Undecided suburban adults in Michigan swing counties worried about cost of living`. Simulant interprets the description into structured filters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the region">
    Narrow by **state or region**, or leave it open for a national cohort.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply demographics">
    Refine by **age range**, **gender**, **income**, **household**, and **education**. Leave a filter as **Any** to keep it open.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add characteristics">
    Select **topic chips** under **Characteristics** — attitudes and dispositions such as `Cost-of-living anxious` or `Climate-focused`. Tags are grounded in source data, not guessed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose cohort size">
    Pick a size preset (500, 1,000, or 2,000) or adjust the slider. Simulant shows the implied precision (±pp) for each size.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name and build">
    Give the cohort a name under **Details**, then click **Build cohort**. Once status is **Ready**, it's available to wire into any simulation step.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Keep cohorts focused. A tight, well-defined cohort produces sharper segment cuts than one broad audience — and you can always build several and compare them.
</Tip>

## Cohort detail

Open any cohort to browse three tabs:

- **Respondents** — the prepared panel, with sortable columns and per-respondent detail
- **Graph** — a 3D view of how respondents cluster
- **Report** — a Deep Research report with sourced narrative about the cohort

## Sample size and precision

As you adjust size, Simulant shows the implied precision (±pp). When you run a step, you choose how many personas to draw from the cohort; the panel is capped at the cohort's total. Larger samples tighten the confidence of quantitative measures; smaller samples run faster.

## Editing and reusing cohorts

Open any cohort from **Cohorts** to adjust its description, filters, or size and **Save changes**. Cohorts are reusable across simulation steps — wire the same cohort node into multiple polls and tests to keep results comparable.
