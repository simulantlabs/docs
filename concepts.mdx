## The object model

<Steps>
  <Step title="Datasets ground the population">
    A **dataset** is a source of real-world signal — census figures, electoral studies, panels, or your own uploaded data. Personas are built from datasets, so every response can be traced back to evidence.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cohorts define who you're reaching">
    A **cohort** is a defined audience — described in plain language, narrowed by region, demographics, and characteristics, and sized to a target precision. It has a respondent count (`n`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assets are what you put in front of them">
    An **asset** is the thing being reacted to — a video, an image, or a block of copy. Assets live in the **Library** section of the sidebar (the route is still `/creatives`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Simulations compose the research">
    A **simulation** is a container for your study. On the **Model** canvas you add **steps** — **Poll**, **Test**, and **Focus group** nodes — and wire them to cohorts, assets, and context. One simulation can mix all three step types.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Objects at a glance

| Object | What it is | Where it lives |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| **Workspace** | The shared space for your team's work, data, and billing | Workspace switcher, top-left |
| **Simulation** | A composed study with polls, tests, and focus groups as steps | **Simulations** |
| **Step** | A single poll, test, or focus group inside a simulation | Simulation **Model** tab |
| **Dataset** | A source of grounding data, versioned with a schema | **Datasets** |
| **Cohort** | A defined, sampled audience | **Cohorts** |
| **Asset** | A media asset or copy to test | **Assets** |
| **Report** | A scheduled or on-demand brief across recent runs | **Reports** |
| **Memo** | A personal or workspace note alongside your research | **Memos** |

## Workspaces

Everything you create belongs to a **workspace**. A workspace has its own datasets, cohorts, simulations, members, plan, and billing. You can belong to more than one and switch between them with the switcher in the top-left of the sidebar. Open **Workspace settings** from the same menu to manage members, tags, and billing. See [Members and roles](/workspace/members).

## Quantitative and qualitative, together

Every test step returns both kinds of feedback:

- **Quantitative** — structured measures such as recall, sentiment, purchase intent, and clarity, scored out of 100 and cut by segment.
- **Qualitative** — open-ended **verbatims** (what individual personas said and why), clustered into **themes**, with **recommendations** distilled on top.

A [focus group step](/guides/focus-groups) adds a third mode: watching a room reason and shift its position live.

Poll steps return **weighted shares** — the fielded percentages a traditional pollster would report — with margins of error and segment cuts.

## Grounding

Simulant models outcomes, not opinions. Personas are built from the measurable record, and every result traces back to the signals behind it — so the read-out survives the room, the audit, and the front page. See [How grounding works](/data/grounding).
