## The object model

<Steps>
  <Step title="Datasets ground the population">
    A **dataset** is a source of observed human evidence — census figures, electoral studies, survey microdata, or your own uploaded data. The empirical foundation is the human record; the language model is not.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cohorts define who you're reaching">
    A **cohort** is a calibrated panel — respondent-backed (anchored to real microdata) or generated inside externally fixed distributions. Composition is set before any generative step runs. It reports respondent count (`n`) and simulation precision.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assets are what you put in front of them">
    An **asset** is the thing being reacted to — a video, an image, or a block of copy. Assets live under **Library → Assets**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Library instruments are reusable designs">
    **Polls**, **Tests**, and **Focus groups** in the Library are reusable instruments. Save a questionnaire or discussion guide once, then load it onto any simulation's Model canvas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Simulations compose the research">
    A **simulation** is a container for your study. On the **Model** canvas you add **steps** — Poll, Test, and Focus group nodes — and wire them to cohorts, assets, and context. One simulation can mix all three step types.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Objects at a glance

| Object | What it is | Where it lives |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| **Workspace** | The shared space for your team's work, data, and billing | Workspace switcher, top-left |
| **Simulation** | A composed study with polls, tests, and focus groups as steps | **Research → Simulations** |
| **Step** | A single poll, test, or focus group inside a simulation | Simulation **Model** tab |
| **Dataset** | A source of grounding data, versioned with a schema | **Library → Datasets** |
| **Cohort** | A defined, sampled audience | **Library → Cohorts** |
| **Asset** | A media asset or copy to test | **Library → Assets** |
| **Poll / Test / Focus group** | A reusable instrument design | **Library → Polls / Tests / Focus groups** |
| **Report** | A scheduled or on-demand brief across recent runs | **Intelligence → Reports** |
| **Memo** | A personal or workspace note alongside your research | **Intelligence → Memos** |
| **Agent** | Plain-language Q&A across your workspace | **Intelligence → Agent** |

## Workspaces

Everything you create belongs to a **workspace**. A workspace has its own datasets, cohorts, simulations, members, plan, and billing. You can belong to more than one and switch between them with the switcher in the top-left of the sidebar. Open **Workspace settings** from the same menu to manage members, tags, and billing. See [Members and roles](/workspace/members).

## Quantitative and qualitative, together

Every test step returns structured measures — such as recall, sentiment, purchase intent, and clarity — scored out of 100 and cut by segment. Poll steps return weighted shares, margins, and segment cuts.

A [focus group step](/guides/focus-groups) adds a qualitative layer: watching a room reason and shift its position live.

Poll steps return **weighted shares** — the fielded percentages a traditional pollster would report — with margins of error and segment cuts.

## Grounding

Synthetic research should not be AI roleplay. It should be a rigorous inference layer on observed human evidence.

**Distributions are deterministic, individuals are generative.** Composition is fixed by official and academic statistics before any language model runs. Representativeness is not validity — a panel can match every demographic target and still answer wrongly, so composition and response behaviour are controlled and reported separately.

See [How grounding works](/data/grounding) and [How cohorts are built](/data/how-cohorts-are-built). For the full specifications, read the white papers on [synthetic research methodology](https://simulant.tech/blog/synthetic-research-methodology/) and [generating synthetic survey respondents](https://simulant.tech/blog/generating-synthetic-respondents/).
