Simulant is a simulation of the people you're trying to reach. It recreates a representative population as grounded personas — modelled on real, official data — so you can put a creative, a message, or a decision in front of them and read the reaction before you commit to it in the real world.

Where traditional research takes a quarter, Simulant runs in a night. You can ask the same population again and again, cut the answer by any segment, and read the verbatims and the reasoning behind every number.

## What you can do

- **Test creative before the spend.** Pre-test ads, hooks, and hero assets across variants and segments.
- **Find the message that lands.** Compare value propositions and read why one wins.
- **Rehearse a launch or a statement.** Stress-test a decision against the room before it goes out.
- **Convene a focus group on demand.** Watch a segment deliberate, react, and shift its mind.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ground a population">
    Simulant builds personas from official datasets — census, electoral studies, social cohesion, household panels, and more. See [How grounding works](/data/grounding).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define who you're reaching">
    Build a [cohort](/guides/cohorts) with a plain-language population description, region, and characteristics.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Compose a simulation">
    Create a [simulation](/guides/simulations), add poll, test, and focus group steps on the Model canvas, and wire them to your cohort and assets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the answer">
    Get quantitative measures, verbatims, themes, and recommendations — cut by any segment.
  </Step>
</Steps>
